Google Summer of Code 2021 - Week #1.
13th June, 2021 | 5 min read
# An Intro!

This is the start of a blog that documents my experience and progress as a GSoC 2021 student with PyMC. The purpose of these blog posts is to keep track of the work being done each week.

The community bonding period was a gratifying experience. I got introduced to various PyMC members and to the great community which I was going to be a part of. Some of the community members gave me helpful pointers on basic things like setting up proper development environments and testing frameworks, especially since I broke my Linux OS by not using virtual environments and tried messing with my system python version 😅

Most of my community bonding time was spent on building the Aesara side of the project. Since I realized that it'll take way more than a week for me to complete(I had allotted the first week of my timetable for this part of the project), plus I had a lot of free time. So I discussed the changes to be made with my mentor @brandonwillard and he gave me a thumbs up regarding the implementation. The implementation itself wasn't hard, maybe a bit lengthy, and hence was the perfect way to start coding for GSoC. Other than that we continued the work from pre-GSoC which involved making a Numba variant of the infamous new RandomVariable Op used in PyMC's v4.0. (#403)

# So what did I do!

The work done by me during Week 1 can be boiled down to the following:

  • - Continued working with Generators in #402 adding tests and more minor fixes.

  • - Refactored a few Distribution classes to be compatible with v4 in #4746 (this also involved making their corresponding RandomVariable classes). This was something that I planned for the second week but put in my current schedule because of #4686.

  • - Continued the work with the Numba variant of RandomVariable Op in #403 .


# Next Week's Plans

Now for week 2, I plan on :

  • - Quickly (or rather hopefully) refactor the few remaining multivariate distributions in #4686 so we can get the alpha version of v4 out.

  • - Refactor the remaining distributions in continous.py, multivariate.py and discrete.py


# A Token of Appreciation 😄

This week has been amazing. Thanks to my ever-supportive mentors Brandon Williard and Thomas Wiecki. Looking forward to an amazing journey ahead.
