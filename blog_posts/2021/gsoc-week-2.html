Google Summer of Code - Week #2

Author - Kaustubh Chaudhari
20th June. 6 min read

Hey there!


Hope you are doing well. This is the second week of my GSoC journey with PyMC. So I am assuming you know about PyMC and the project, if not, do check out PyMC and my previous blog post about my GSoC project.


Progress this week:


This week, it was planned that we'll be refactoring the remaining distributions from continous.py, discrete.py, and multivariate.py, however much of these distributions were already refactored thanks to people from the community. And since #4686 was being set up for an alpha release, I refactored the remaining distributions from continous.py in #4746. Hence this week, I worked on refactoring the multivariate distributions from #4686.


The specific distributions that I worked on are as follows :

  • Wishart and MatrixNormal distribution (#4777): The refactoring work here is mostly done. The only thing holding it back is a ndim_supp issue (link to comment) with the Wishart distribution. The ndim_supp was introduced in v4 as a sanity check for shapes of distribution samples. This comment explains it's work in detail: link to comment

  • LKJCorr and _LKJCholeskyCov (#4784): The work here can be marked as work in progress, the issue with this refactoring is implementing fancy indexing symbolically (link to comment). Other than that, a few tests will be needed and we'll be good to go.

  • KroneckerNormal distribution (#4774): This particular class faces major design issues, as its logp method relied heavily on object-oriented instance variables in v3. And this is something we're trying to avoid in v4, by passing those variables as arguments and essentially making dist and logp class methods, hence extending their workability without the need to define an entire instance of a distribution class. Implementing a v4 version of this distribution with its full capabilities might require some out-of-the-box hacks like this and this one . Regardless, if the current issue with the conversion of instance variables to arguments (as TensorConstants and TensorVariables) is resolved the rest of the refactoring won't be much of a problem.


What's Next:


The next week I plan on discussing and refactoring the Mixture class, which will be another challenge in itself and complete the refactoring in #4774 and #4784. And hopefully this week we can complete the targets from #4686.


A Note of Thanks:


Thanks to the amazing mentors and contributors in the community for helping me out with suggestions and reviews.
